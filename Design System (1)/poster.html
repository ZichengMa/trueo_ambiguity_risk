TrueO: Ambiguity Risk Scoring for Prediction Market Prompts

RAG retrieval + web search deliver expert-level accuracy and interpretable rationales across all difficulty levels
Background

Prediction markets let people trade on real-world outcomes, where resolution depends on clear prompts defining what counts as a win or loss. Yet many prompts are ambiguous or underspecified, causing disputes, inconsistent outcomes, and reduced trust. Our mission is to build an AI system that detects ambiguity pre-launch, assigns a risk score, and suggests clear revisions to ensure fair and reliable markets.

Objectives
  • Detect ambiguity — unclear time windows, vague terms, missing resolution sources, and subjective conditions in market prompts
  • Generate rewrites — add explicit dates, time zones, definitions, and authoritative resolution sources while preserving original intent
Example
⚠ Ambiguous
"Will the Fed raise rates next quarter?"
Missing: exact date · rate definition · official source
✓ TrueO Rewrite
"Will the FOMC raise the federal funds target rate at its May 7, 2025 meeting, per the official FOMC press release?"
How It Works
  • GLM-4.7 reads the prompt with an expert scoring rubric and outputs a structured risk assessment
  • RAG retrieval finds the most similar past examples from our labeled corpus and provides them as scoring anchors
  • Web search (Tavily) checks official sources and definitions to determine if apparent ambiguity resolves in context
System Pipeline
  • Input Augmentation — RAG retrieves top-5 low-risk and top-5 high-risk examples via TF-IDF as scoring anchors; Tavily web search adds site-biased results reranked by authority (.gov/.edu > official > media > community)
  • Output — risk_score (0–100 severity) · risk_tags (7 categories flagging issue types) · rationale (human-readable explanation) · rewrite (a rewrite suggestion without ambiguity)
Data
  • 200 scraped labeled market prompts by the team, serving as the RAG retrieval corpus — each annotated with a ground-truth risk score, expert rationale, and market link
  • 30-sample stratified evaluation set — 10 easy · 10 medium · 10 hard prompts. Test questions excluded from retrieval to prevent data leakage.
Evaluation

Baseline: bare GLM-4.7, no rubric, no examples, no web search. SocREval (He et al., 2023) — reference-free LLM-as-judge on four 1–5 dimensions:

  • Score Accuracy — predicted score proximity to ground truth
  • Rationale Quality — matches expert-identified ambiguity issues
  • Tag Correctness — tags consistent with identified issues
  • Overall Quality — holistic assessment

Conclusions
  • A working end-to-end pipeline. TrueO achieves a 71.5% reduction in scoring error against expert ground truth — demonstrating that automated ambiguity assessment is feasible at production quality.
  • RAG + web search — retrieved examples calibrate the scoring scale; live search distinguishes true underspecification from domain unfamiliarity.
